# Findings: Baseline LLM Hallucination on Contingency Analysis

When presented with the query:
"Can you run AC power flow on the IEEE 118-bus grid to find the top 5 most heavily loaded lines, then run contingency analysis on those and summarize any operational limit violations?"

The system produces two different responses based on the pipeline used.

## 1. Baseline Pipeline (LLM Only)
**Output:**
- Line 1 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 2 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 3 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 4 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 5 outage: no operational limit violations.

**Analysis:**
The baseline LLM **hallucinates/guesses** the results of the contingency analysis. Because the baseline agent does not have access to any external tools (like `run_power_flow` or `run_n1_contingency`), it only sees the precomputed evidence from the *base case* network. It has no mechanism to actually take lines out of service or compute the post-contingency power flow. Consequently, it fabricates a plausible-looking response (assuming lines 1-5 are the top 5 and guessing "no violations" based on low base-case loading) to satisfy the strict formatting instructions of the prompt.

## 2. Agentic Pipeline (With Tools)
**Output:**
- Line 6 outage: 3 voltage violations (bus 8 at 0.0 pu < 0.94 pu, bus 9 at 0.0 pu < 0.94 pu, bus 37 at 0.9386 pu < 0.94 pu); no branch overloads.
- Line 7 outage: 2 voltage violations (bus 9 at 0.0 pu < 0.94 pu, bus 37 at 0.9386 pu < 0.94 pu); no branch overloads.
- Line 34 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 89 outage: no operational limit violations.
- Line 90 outage: no operational limit violations.

**Analysis:**
The Agentic Pipeline successfully utilizes its provided tools. It first queries the network to accurately identify the actual top 5 most heavily loaded lines (Lines 6, 7, 34, 89, 90). It then sequentially triggers the `run_n1_contingency` simulation tool for each line, inspecting the deterministic voltage and loading violations returned by the physics engine. Finally, it constructs the exact requested strings based on real, simulated data rather than guessing.
